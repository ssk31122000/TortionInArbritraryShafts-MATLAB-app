Introduction - Torsion (for arbitrary shafts)

We see the application of torsion everyday in our life. Torsionally loaded shafts are the common structures used in machine design. Daily life examples of torsion include hinged doors, tightening/loosening nuts using wrench, carâ€™s steering, turning of tap, wringing of wet clothes by hand, etc.

Torsion is a twisting/deforming of a shaft about its longitudinal axis by an applied twisting moment or torque. One typical engineering example of torsionally loaded members is the drive shaft of an automobile. It transmits power from prime mover (engine) to the drive wheels.
