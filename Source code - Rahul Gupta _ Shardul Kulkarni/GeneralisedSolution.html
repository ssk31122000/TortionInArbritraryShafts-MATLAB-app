Concepts of non circular cross section shafts - Torsion (for arbitrary shafts)

St. Venant Principle:

Stress distribution lines distort from straight paths when lines are near the end of the surface of loading. St. Venant principle states that, “The stress distribution lines located away from the load and support will remain straight/undistorted.”

Assumptions of generalised method for arbitrary cross sections:

  1. Linear Elastic range.
  2. Isotropic materials.
  3. St. Venant principle should be applicable.
  4. Applied Shear stresses should generate twisting moment or other way round.
  5. Prismatic beam, i.e., cross section along the length of the shaft is uniform.
  6. Each cross section will go rigid body rotation.
  7. Small Displacements.
  8. There can be distortion of cross section in context of warping.
  9. Negligible poisson effects, Shape of cross section will remain same.
  10. Zero Body forces.
