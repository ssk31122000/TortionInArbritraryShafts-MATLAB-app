Concepts of non circular cross section shafts - Torsion (for arbitrary shafts)

Many prismatic shafts have circular cross sections, but sometimes it is sometimes required to design shafts of non- circular cross sections (elliptic, triangular, I shaped) or circular cross sections having holes in it.

Examples:

  1. Square, Flat, round, kennedy, woodruff types of keys holes in shaft-hub arrangement.
  2. Brackets and supports loaded with torsion.

Design of non-circular cross sections is required in the above two examples.

The calculations of stresses and displacements for the torsion of a non-circular shaft involve quite complex equations, it needs to be solved through a general approach. The assumptions for circular cross-sections should not apply for non-circular shafts, as the plane sections perpendicular to the longitudinal axis do not remain plane post twisting. During twisting, deformation happens in the axial direction and the torsional hills / bulges are generated on the cross section which is called warping.
